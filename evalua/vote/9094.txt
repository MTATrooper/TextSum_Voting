burying the dead along with a human sacrifice was common in ancient korea . the skeletons were found on top of one another in the tomb from korea's silla dynasty , leading some experts suggesting that the set up could even have been designed to show two people having sex . an unusual tomb dating to between the 5 th and 6 th century seems to hold the remains of two lovers , one of whom may have been killed so the pair could lie together for eternity . here , the bones of the two people can be seen on top of one another , leading some experts to suggest the set up was designed to imitate sex . archaeologists are almost certain that the tomb was intended for a noblewoman and believe that the man she is lying with may have been killed so he could be buried with her . both the woman and the man in the tomb are estimated to be between the ages of 20 and 30 when they died , or were killed .
