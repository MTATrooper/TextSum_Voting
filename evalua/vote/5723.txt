a new comprehensive study of 3.9 million military personnel who served after 9/11 has found no direct link between deployment to a war zone and elevated suicide rates . researchers at joint base lewis mcchord in tacoma , washington , concluded that military suicides may be more likely after members leave the service than during active duty deployment , particularly if their time in uniform is brief . the four year study , the largest of its kind , was published wednesday in the journal of american medicine association psychiatry .
